Why Software Development?

One of my favorite shows as a kid was Dexter's Laboratory. Dexter was a genius who built a huge lab to do experiments and build his various devices. Every episode was categorized by some problem that Dexter or his family had to face. The recurring theme was Dexter would have to use some device in his lab to solve the problem. No matter what the problem was he could always invent something to solve it. I loved this show as a child because I loved how Dexter could tackle any problem with the inventions in his lab. I love that he used his technical skills to conquer a seemingly infinite array of problems. This is one of the reasons I chose and love Software Development. Software Developers have the skills to change the world and there is a seemingly infinite number of problems that can be solved through software.


There was often a caveat however to Dexterâ€™s problem solving inventions. None of his inventions worked perfectly. There was always some oversight or flaw he had to overcome in the process. This is seen in software development as well. In software development there is a never a perfect solution to any problem. There are risks and tradeoffs to be made when developing anything. Failure is inevitable along this path but good software developers fail gracefully. That is, we fail and learn from them iteratively over and over again becoming better each time. This is something I try to remember when I code and I remember that even though Dexter always fought difficulty while building his contraptions he was always able to learn from his failures and make something that got the job done.

iAdvisor Web App

iAdvisor was started in my web design class as a class projects. It has and continues to evolve into a functioning web app and the hope is it will help resolve the problems the InfoSci department faces by streamlining the four year planning process and allowing students to quickly and efficiently build thier schedule and allow advisors to view many students at a glance and provide feedback. The goal is for advisors to be able to accommodate many students at once and for students to have the freedom of four year planning at thier convenience.

Mapping Inequality Database Design and Data Cleaning

The Mapping Inequality project is meant to use data to display and analyze how redlining in the U.S. affected neighborhoods and housing communities. The data was given to our team in extremely unorganized JSON format and was cleaned and structured using python and the pandas library.
